Urban Flood Early Warning Using Data-Driven Models

This research develops data-driven and optimization-based flood early warning frameworks that improve the timeliness, reliability, and interpretability of urban flood risk assessment by integrating machine learning, probabilistic modeling, and hydrodynamic simulation.

  • Probabilistic rainfall–inundation modeling: Developed a data-driven framework using modified Support Vector Machines (SVMs) to estimate probabilities of flash flooding, replacing binary rainfall-threshold warnings with probabilistic risk outputs.
  • Rainfall pattern analysis for hazardous event identification: Introduced hydrological rainfall pattern analysis to identify critical short-duration, high-intensity rainfall events, improving training data quality and early-warning reliability.
  • Optimization of rainfall thresholds for pluvial flood warnings: Applied tabu search optimization to efficiently calibrate rainfall thresholds for urban flood warnings and warning issuance 1–3 hours in advance.

These methods demonstrate how machine learning, optimization algorithms, and hybrid data sources can support efficient, scalable, and interpretable flood early warning systems for urban environments.

Interactive Geo-Visualization for Urban Flood Early Warning Systems

This work develops a web-based flood early warning and visualization system that integrates hydrological modeling, time-series correction, and real-time monitoring data to support timely flood risk awareness and decision-making.

  • Integrated flood forecasting pipeline: Combined rainfall–runoff modeling, 1D river routing, and storm surge boundary conditions to simulate future water stages across hundreds of river cross-sections.
  • Real-time geo-visualization and alerts: Developed a web-based platform that visualizes predicted water stages, flood depths, real-time rainfall, monitoring stations, and CCTV feeds, with automatic warning signals triggered when critical thresholds are exceeded.
  • Operational decision support: Enabled continuous, automated model execution and browser-based access, providing actionable lead time for flood preparedness and emergency response in urban river basins.
