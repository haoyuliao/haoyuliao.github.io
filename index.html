Dr. Hao-Yu (Howard) Liao

Postdoctoral Researcher | Institute of Food and Agricultural Sciences (IFAS) | University of Florida

Dr. Hao-Yu Liao is a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Florida, specializing in integrating artificial intelligence with robotics, geospatial data, and environmental systems. His research focuses on enhancing human-robot collaboration for sustainable remanufacturing and disassembly operations through intelligent automation. With an academic background in environmental engineering, industrial engineering, and computer science, Dr. Liao develops AI-based solutions to tackle real-world challenges involving risk mitigation, repairability prediction, and robotic decision-making. His innovative work combines deep learning, sensor integration, and optimization algorithms to optimize robotic safety and precision in industrial applications.

Beyond robotics, Dr. Liao leads efforts in Geospatial Artificial Intelligence (GeoAI) for ecosystem service assessments, developing scalable pipelines for analyzing remote sensing data using Contrastive Languageâ€“Image Pre-training and Large Foundation Models. He has successfully applied multimodal foundation models and zero-shot learning to map recreational activities and assess cultural services at regional scales.

His contributions to machine learning in environmental and robotic applications have been recognized with numerous honors, including the DFMLC Best Paper Award and the UF Postdoctoral Travel Award. He also serves as a reviewer for over 15 international journals across robotics, AI, environmental science, and geoinformatics.
