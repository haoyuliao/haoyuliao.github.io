Dr. Hao-Yu (Howard) Liao

Postdoctoral Researcher | Institute of Food and Agricultural Sciences (IFAS) | University of Florida

I earned my Ph.D. in Engineering School of Sustainable Infrastructure & Environment from the University of Florida, with a research focus on robotics and artifical intelligent for sustainable remanufacturing. My work integrated deep learning, IMU sensors, and optimization models to support repairability prediction, human–robot collaboration, and safety evaluation in disassembly systems.

I currently serve as a Postdoctoral Researcher at UF, where I apply Geospatial Artificial Intelligence (GeoAI) to environmental monitoring and ecosystem service assessment, using remote sensing and foundation models to analyze human–environment interactions.

My research has been recognized with the DFMLC Best Paper Award and the UF Postdoctoral Travel Award. I actively review for over 15 international journals across robotics, AI, and environmental science.
